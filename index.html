The Wedding

Ceremony details

Wedding ceremony at 5p, Sunday June 14th, 2015 at The Rooftop at the Downtown Banque Lofts (219 Central Ave NW, Albuquerque NM 87102). Reception, dinner, and dancing to follow.



The venue is a rooftop in downtown Albuquerque. There should be ample free parking on surrounding streets. The weather will be warm and sunny! We encourage hats and hot weather clothing.

Bart adds: The entrance to the Sunrise Bank Building/venue is rather inconspicuous even though it consists of sizable double doors. It is, however, right next to a visible Chicago Dog restaurant, same address.

Rehearsal Dinner

Our rehearsal dinner will take place at 6:30p on Saturday, 6/13 at High Noon restaurant (425 San Felipe St NW).

Hotel

CROWNE PLAZA

Our wedding hotel is the Crowne Plaza, Albuquerque at 1901 University Boulevard. Rooms are $99.00 per night, which includes free parking (but not breakfast).

Book your rooms online here or call the hotel directly at 505-884-2500 and ask for Tony Ration. Please make your reservations before May 31.

Judith says: If you try to make a reservation and are told that the room block is full, or that the hotel doesn't have the type of room you want, don't worry! They have plenty of rooms, they just need to add more to our block. If you need additional help, you can email either of us, call me (505-401-4033), or call the room block manager directly: Tony at 505-998-6405. Thank you for supporting the Brillman-Wohl wedding!

If you do not make your reservation through the online link, please specify that you are part of the Brillman-Wohl Wedding block.



ROOMS

You can book two types of rooms at the Crowne Plaza

  • High Rise Rooms:are noisier, but have great views.
  • Cabana Rooms: are quieter, low rise rooms. You can specifically ask for a cabana room facing the courtyard (these rooms have patios or small balconies).
Please specify the type of room you'd prefer when making your reservation.

Directions

To the Crowne Plaza

Directions for driving to Crowne Plaza Hotel from airport rental car pavilion: All rental cars leave from a central rental car pavilion which you access by shuttle bus at the airport. For directions to the Crowne Plaza from locations other than the Albuquerque Airport, please click the "directions" icon on the map above.

  • Once in your car, exit rental car lot
  • Follow signs to Albuquerque/I-25 N. ( a right/left/right and you are on I-25 N)
  • Continue on I-95 for 2.7 miles.
  • Take exit 225, ''Lomas, Menaul, Candelaria, local traffic''. This is Oak St, which becomes the North Frontage Road.
  • Continue 0.9 miles on the North Frontage Road until you are literally under the freeway interchange.
  • Turn right on the East Frontage Road.
  • Make the first left, University. You will cross under I-40. (If you don’t turn you will merge on onto I-40)
  • The Crowne Plaza is the first left after the I-40 entrance ramp

To the Brillman-Wohl home (4611 Royene Ave, NE)

Directions for driving to the Brillman-Wohl house (4611 Royene Ave, NE) from the Crowne Plaza Hotel.:

  • Turn right (S) out of hotel parking lot onto University.
  • Go under I-40 to first left onto I-40 East, Santa Rosa
  • Go 0.8 miles to exit 160, Carlisle Blvd
  • Turn right onto Carlisle Blvd, go 0.3 miles
  • Make the first left, at the light, onto Indian School Rd.
  • Go 0.5 miles to the next light.
  • Turn right onto Washington St.
  • Go 0.3 miles to Royene and make a left.
  • (the sign for Royene on the left is obscured by a tree- you will see a sign for Royene on the right a few yards before you can make a left on Royene)
  • We are at the first corner (Jefferson) on the left. It’s the house with the circular drive.

Directions to Rehearsal Dinner

Directions for driving to High Noon (425 San Felipe St NW) from the Crowne Plaza Hotel:

  • Turn Right (S) out of hotel parking lot onto University.
  • Take first right, I-40 west frontage road west 1.3 miles
  • Merge onto I-40 west and go 1.1 miles
  • Take Exit 157A Rio Grande Blvd
  • Make an immediate left under I-40 onto Rio Grande Blvd
  • Travel 0.5 miles
  • Make a left onto Mountain Rd.
  • On the right , you will see a small turquoise sign “High Noon” mounted on an adobe brick wall, next to a wooden arch. This is the entrance to the High Noon parking lot
  • Turn right into the parking lot. (If you hit San Felipe Street, you have missed the lot)
  • Walk out of the parking lot at the opposite end from the driveway.
  • High Noon is on the left. The entrance is on Charlevoix St. Even though the address is on San Felipe St; the restaurant is at the corner of Charlevoix St and San Felipe St

Parking at High Noon: Be careful when parking in the lot, because the it is poorly graded. If there are no parking spaces left in the lot or surrounding streets, there is a free parking garage 2 blocks further on the left.

ALBUQUERQUE

FOR THE OUTDOORSY

  • Sandia Peak Tramway: Fly across 2.7 miles of mountains and canyons, hike at the end.
  • Albuquerque BioPark: Complete with a zoo, aquarium, botanical gardens, and something that the city has decided to call a "beach."
  • Rio Grande Nature Center State Park: Birdwatching, wildlife viewing, and trail access to the Rio Grande.

FOR THE INDOORSY

  • Sandia Peak Tramway: There's a restaurant and nature center with great views up there, too.
  • Albuquerque Old Town: What Albuquerque was like in 1709, but with electricity, air conditioning, and way better food.
  • ABQ Trolley's Commercial Breaking Bad Tour: Because you're going to a wedding in a city best known for a fictional meth dealer.
  • Albuquerque Museum: "One of the Southwest's premier cultural destinations." Don't shake your head like that. They've got a sculpture garden, too.
